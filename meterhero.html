MeterHero



Since August of 2015 I have worked as a Software Engineer for MeterHero, a utility conservation start-up located in Oakland, California. Over the course of my time there, I have built several administrative features, including a user tagging system utilizing the Intercom API and a utility assigning system for users.


As 50% of the development team, I helped to build a "Communities" feature for MeterHero. The company had decided to make a change in it's business model, going from offering sponsored rebates to users that conserved water and energy, to becoming a social network of sorts, giving users the ability to join communities where they could track their utility data alongside others. In the course of 2 weeks, we re-purposed old and unused tables in our schema, and created a front facing feature using Ember.js, a framework that neither of us had any experience with. In the end, we succeeded in launching the feature that would become the forefront of MeterHero's new mission, "Communities."



As a design-centric programmer, I was also thrilled to be able to redesign the entire front page of the website twice. Both times, I worked closely with our Design/UX lead to create a mobile-responsive front page that featured hand-drawn assets and utilized both flex-box and Sass.
